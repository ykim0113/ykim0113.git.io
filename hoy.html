Hoy

Focus on today to accomplish long-term goals.

Overview

During my sophomore year summer break, I came across my friend’s problem which I emphasized a lot with as a fellow college student. He was worried about becoming a Junior and having to balance hard classes with work and social life. He has been using productivity apps to stay organized but he said they didn’t help him too much to accomplish his long term goals.

My friend had an idea of for making a new version of a productivity app and I wanted to help him contextualize the problem and come up with a viable solution. The initial outcome was to build a productivity app for long-term goals and for me was to design user-friendly features and design system of the app’s UI.

Duration

Role: UI/UX Design

Jun - Jul 2018

Tools: Sketch, InVision

User Research

I spent a week researching about potential users of the app. Although the primary user of the app was my friend (Computer Science major at Georgia Tech), I wanted to broaden our users to students at Georgia Tech as 67.5% of students uses productivity apps. My research encompassed:

  • Survey

  • Market Research

Survey

I asked my teammate and couple more friends about their experiences of using a productivity app or planner through a Google Form to collect qualitative data. Some questions I asked were:

  • Past experience using productivity apps

  • How user would define long-term and short-term goal (duration vs frequency)

  • How users manage their tasks (prioritizing, categorizing, number of lists)

Market Research

I conducted a market research on existing productivity and time management apps. From this research I was able to determine that our app would be a combination of project management and productivity apps.

Ideation

From the insights, two big design choices I made were focusing on the usability of the app and designing a UI element system that would reflect these design choices.

Design Challenge 1: Usability of the app

Sitemap; I started creating a sitemap and low-fi wireframes for primary use cases.

  • Put milestones and tasks for the day on a single list for the day so the user can have a holistic view of their day and not switch back and forth screens with only milestones and with only tasks.

  • Use detailed logs for a long-term goal to decrease the user’s burden of having to remember where they left off in the previous milestone.

Design Challenge 2: UI Elements Design

As I was the only UI/UX designer of the app, I was responsible for designing the whole UI components of the app. Following Material Design guideline, I customized the component so they will be consistent across the entire app and align with the mood I wanted to capture.

Over 2 months, I came up with 4 design themes as I was developing the lo-fi wireframe.

To capture a clean, calm, organized feeling for the user experience of the app, I used:

  • White and light blue color scheme
  • Roboto typeface to ensure legibility and readability
  • Logo for the starting screen to complete the branding of the app.

EVALUATION

Due to limitations on time and location, I could not fully conduct full usability tests on Hoy. However I let two of my interview participants try out the prototype and they reacted positively. In addition their feedbacks helped improving the UI for the main features of the app.
